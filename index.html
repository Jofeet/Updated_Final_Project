About Olongapo

Olongapo City is a first-class city located in the province of Zambales, Central Luzon, Philippines. It is known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and natural beauty. The city is a popular destination for both local and foreign tourists, offering a wide range of activities and attractions, from beautiful beaches and scenic mountain trails to historical landmarks and bustling markets.

The Legend of
Olongapo City

Once upon a time, in a village along Subic Bay, lived a leader named Apo, known for
his love, intelligence, and fairness. His village became a model of justice and
goodwill, inspiring neighboring settlements. However, envious men abducted and
killed Apo. His head was found impaled on a bamboo pole, sparking outrage among
the villagers. They punished the culprits and named their village Olongapo, meaning
"head of the chieftain," in honor of Apo's leadership.

About the
City Mayor

Rolen C. Paulino Jr. is a successful examinee of the 2015 Bar Examinations. He completed his Bachelor of Laws in San Beda in 2015 ranking 29th out of 220 graduates. He obtained his pre-law degree from Ateneo De Manila University in 2011.

He has been exposed to various legal internships and employment, giving him invaluable hands-on experiences and practical know-how appearing in court as counsel in various courts and tribunals, dealing with clients, conducting legal researches and drafting pleadings, orders and court decisions during his internships. On May of 2019, he was elected as City Mayor of Olongapo City, Zambales.
